Short Bio

I am a Senior Lecturer in Aerospace Engineering at Swansea University. My research focuses on cryogenic hydrogen thermofluids, multiphase CFD, digital twins, hydrogen safety, combustion, advanced propulsion and sustainable aviation.

I lead the development of a high-fidelity CFD and digital-twin platform for multiphase, multi-species, reacting and non-reacting thermofluid flows under wide-ranging temperature, pressure and flow-speed conditions. My work has been applied to gas turbines, rocket engines, scramjet engines, detonation engines, cryogenic storage and transport, thermal management systems, fuel safety and hazard assessment.

Before joining Swansea, I was an Assistant Professor at Northwestern Polytechnical University and a Research Fellow at the University of Warwick, contributing to the EU-funded PRESLHY project on the safe use of liquid hydrogen.

Research Highlights

My research lies at the interface of aerospace propulsion, hydrogen and ammonia fuels, cryogenic thermofluids, clean-energy systems and digital engineering, with highlighted themes:

  • Cryogenic hydrogen and fuel safety: liquefaction, condensation, ignition, combustion, dispersion and risk mitigation for hydrogen infrastructure, aviation, maritime and mobile applications.
  • Multiphase CFD and digital twins: high-fidelity simulation, reduced-order modelling, uncertainty-aware prediction and physics-informed machine learning for energy and propulsion systems.
  • Advanced propulsion and detonation physics: rotating detonation, oblique detonation, shock-wave interactions, supersonic reactive flow and pressure-gain combustion.
  • Thermal management and cryogenic heat exchangers: heat exchangers for hydrogen-electric propulsion, clean energy systems, batteries and cross-sector decarbonisation.
